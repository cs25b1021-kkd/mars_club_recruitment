**MAZE ROVER** 
it's an autonomous maze solving rover.
it's the same that used for testing the my maze rover for savara fest.

components used -
3 ultrasonic sensors, for finding obstacle(wall) distance.
L293D motor driver, controlling motors.
2 motors, for movement of rover.
arduino uno, micro-contorller.

In this project, different wire colors were used to clearly distinguish between various types of connections.
This made the circuit easier to understand and debug.

🔴 Red wires were used for all power connections, including battery positive and 5V supply lines.
⚫ Black wires were used for ground (GND) connections throughout the circuit.
🟤 Brown wires were used for motor output connections from the motor driver to the motors.
🟢 Green wires were used for enable (PWM) pins, which control the motor speed.
🔵 Blue wires were used for echo pins of the ultrasonic sensors.
🟡 Yellow wires were used for trigger pins of the ultrasonic sensors.
🟣 Purple wires were used for input control pins connected from the Arduino to the motor driver.

This color-coding approach helped in reducing wiring errors and made the overall layout
more organized and visually clear during simulation in TinkerCAD.
